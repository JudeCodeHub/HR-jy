# Master HR - Application Flow Guide

Welcome! Your Master HR app is now running correctly. Here is how the system works and how you can test the different flows.

## 1. Initial Setup (Sign Up)
To get started, you first need to create an HR account.
1. On the first screen, click **"Sign Up"**.
2. Fill in your details to create an HR Administrator account.
3. Once registered, you can log in using your email and password.

## 2. HR Management Flow (Admin)
The HR manager is the "brain" of the system.
- **Login**: Select "HR" as the role on the login screen.
- **Add Employees**: Go to "Manage Employees" to register new staff.
  - **Important**: When you add an employee, you upload their photo. This photo is used by the **Face Recognition** module later.
- **Set Payroll & Attendance**: You can set basic salaries, allowances, and allowed leaves for each employee.
- **Assign Tasks**: Use the Task Management section to send tasks to specific employees.
- **View Performance**: Monitor how many tasks employees complete and their attendance records.

## 3. Employee Flow (Staff)
This is what your employees will use on their own phones.
- **Login**: Select "Employee" as the role. Use the email and password the HR created for them.
- **Mark Attendance (Face Recognition)**: 
  - This is the core feature! Employees click "Mark Attendance".
  - The app uses **Geofencing** to check if the employee is actually at the office (within a certain radius).
  - Then, it launches the camera. The photo is sent to your **Python Face Recognition & Antispoofing** service.
  - The Python service checks if the face matches the employee's ID and ensures it's a "Real" person (not a photo of a photo).
- **View Tasks**: Employees can see the tasks assigned by the HR and mark them as completed.
- **Chat**: Integrated chat to communicate with the HR manager.

## 4. How the Backend Services Work Together
- **Node.js Server (Port 3501)**: Handles all database operations (MongoDB), user accounts, and general app logic.
- **Python Face Recognition (Port 5000)**: Used exclusively when an employee tries to mark attendance. It processes the camera image and returns a "Match" or "Fail" result.
- **Antispoofing**: Part of the Python service that detects if someone is trying to trick the system with a fake image.

## 5. Summary Checklist for Testing
1. Ensure your **Backend (index.js)** and **Python (App.py)** are both running in your terminals.
2. Sign up as an **HR**.
3. Log in as HR and **Add an Employee** (use a real photo).
4. Log out and log in as the **Employee** you just created.
5. Try to **Mark Attendance** (ensure you are close to the location set in the system).
